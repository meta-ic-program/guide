What is an Independent Contractor?

Understanding the definition and why Meta uses a structured program.

An Independent Contractor (IC) is a self-employed individual or small business with fewer than five employees that provides specialized services. Unlike employees or W2 contractors, ICs operate their own business, control how and when work is performed, typically use their own tools and methods, are responsible for their own taxes and benefits, and provide services to multiple clients — not just Meta.

Important: US law is very specific about who qualifies as an IC. Misclassification can expose Meta to significant legal, tax, and financial risks. All IC engagements must go through MBO Partners.

Why Meta uses the IC Program

Compliance

Ensures every engagement meets IRS and state classification standards.

Protection

Shields CWAMs and Meta from misclassification penalties.

Simplicity

Streamlines vetting, contracting, and onboarding through a single vendor.

Who is MBO Partners?

MBO Partners is an industry leader in Independent Contractor compliance solutions and a trusted partner of Meta's. As Meta's approved vendor, they specialize in IC compliance and engagement management — handling the full IC process on Meta's behalf, including worker eligibility verification, onboarding, insurance requirements, compliance checks, contracts, and payments. MBO brings deep expertise in US IC rules and regulations, ensuring every engagement is defensible and audit-ready.

Step-by-Step Process

From intake to first day. Plan for a minimum of 15 business days — responsiveness from both the CWAM and the IC is the biggest factor in timing.

Tip: Engage early. Allow a minimum 3-week lead time. Connect with your Finance BP before starting to validate budget codes (Project Code, Cost Code, Product Code).

Phase 1 — Initiation in Buy@

1
Complete the Global Intake Form in Buy@CWAM

Access Buy@ via People Portal and complete the Global Intake Form (Supplier Invite Form + Third Party Assessment / TPA). You will need: project scope, deliverables and cost, supplier name and contact details, worker pay type, cost center, project code, estimated start/end dates, and whether the supplier needs site/systems access or will handle sensitive data.

2
Supplier completes registrationSupplier

The supplier receives the Supplier Invite Form and confirms their number of employees. If fewer than 5 employees, they are routed to the Business Validation queue in Buy@ for Lara Rustom to action and engage MBO Partners.

3
Legal review (if TPA flagged)Meta Legal

If the Third Party Assessment is flagged, Meta Legal reviews the engagement. Once approved, Lara requests a referral for engagement by MBO Partners.

Phase 2 — MBO Evaluation & Onboarding

4
Manager & supplier complete MBO questionnairesCWAM + Supplier

Both the CWAM and the supplier complete structured questionnaires (Manager Questionnaire and Supplier Enrollment Form / SEF). MBO uses these responses to determine the appropriate engagement pathway. Allow approximately 45 minutes total.

5
MBO presents evaluation resultsMBO + CWAM

MBO reviews the responses, determines the engagement service (Vendor Services, Business Services, or Payroll Services), and presents the evaluation results and engagement cost to the CWAM for approval.

6
Worker completes onboarding checklistSupplier

MBO provides the worker with a detailed daily list of onboarding steps. The worker completes: Meta-required background check, Meta onboarding documents (I-9 for W2, MBO account creation, business documentation such as Articles of Incorporation, W-9, proof of other clients). MBO assists with LLC setup if needed.

7
MBO creates Beeline request; Finance approvesMBO + Finance

MBO creates the Beeline requisition and submits the candidate to the CWAM and Finance for approval. Once Finance approves, the background check step is completed and the request routes to the CWx team for provisioning.

Phase 3 — Provisioning & Start

8
AGS/CWx provisioning and setupAGS / CWx

The CWx team handles provisioning and setup in Workday. If the worker requires equipment, the CWx team initiates the CW Profile process (see Equipment section below).

9
SOW signed; worker cleared to startMBO + Worker

MBO initiates the Statement of Work (SOW) process and sends it to Meta and the worker for e-signature. MBO also provides the worker with detailed instructions for submitting milestones, deliverables, and expenses.

Important: Workers cannot begin their project until all evaluation and onboarding steps are fully complete. MBO Partners will confirm once everything is in order.

Pre-Start Checklist

›Third Party Assessment (TPA) completed
›IC Evaluation by MBO completed
›IC Background Check completed
›SOW / IC Agreement signed
›Beeline Work Order approved by Finance
›Worker Facebook URL check completed

Equipment & Setup

Equipment can only ship after an active Beeline assignment, Workday profile, and confirmed shipping address.

Tip: Plan ahead. Shipping takes approximately 10 business days. Consider setting the project start date one week earlier than needed so the worker has equipment on day one.

Ordering Equipment

Equipment is ordered through the Enterprise Store (Buy@). Navigate to Your devices in the left menu, then select the Laptop or Phone tile. Fill out the request details and choose your shipping method.

Open Enterprise Store (Buy@)

Worker Setup Process

1

Activate CW Profile

The worker receives an email from Meta Enterprise Center (notification@mail.facebookenterprise.com) prompting them to activate their Contingent Worker Profile. This email can land in spam — the worker should check their junk folder and alert MBO Client Care immediately if it is not received.

2

Provide shipping address

After logging into the CW Profile (metaenterprise.com), the worker provides their shipping address. The address must be within the country of employment. Equipment is estimated to arrive within 10 business days of address entry.

3

Equipment setup

The first-time setup password is included in the equipment tracking email, along with a link to the setup portal. For device setup support, the worker can call 1-650-644-5000 or use the Helpdesk on Workplace.

Equipment Returns

At the end of the assignment, the worker receives an email directing them to their CW Profile page to initiate the return. If no email is received, they should go to metaenterprise.com/cw/ directly.

Return process:

1.Select Start your return on the Equipment returns page
2.Choose Return or Dispute for each asset
3.Confirm assets are not hazardous
4.Select return method: UPS QR Code (recommended) or courier drop-off
5.Enter shipping address and submit
Important: Failure to return Meta assets at the end of an assignment can result in a financial penalty.

Invoicing & Payments

The process differs slightly depending on the worker's engagement type, but your role as CWAM is consistent: approve submissions in Beeline.

IC / Milestone Billers

1.Worker submits milestone invoices into MBO Portal / JobDiva by Sunday night
2.MBO pulls milestones and submits into Beeline as Miscellaneous Invoices on Monday mornings
3.CWAM approves in Beeline by Thursday 8 PM ET
4.Invoice consolidated Friday; Meta pays MBO at Net 15; MBO pays worker ~3 weeks from consolidation

Payroll / Hourly Billers

1.Worker submits time directly into Beeline each week by Sunday night
2.CWAM approves time in Beeline by Thursday 8 PM ET
3.Invoice consolidated Friday; Meta pays MBO at Net 15; MBO pays worker 1–2 weeks from consolidation (varies by state)
Important: CWAMs must approve all Beeline submissions by Thursday at 8 PM ET. Workers cannot be paid until approval is made. If you need a delegate approver (e.g., you are on leave), a backup can be assigned in Beeline.
